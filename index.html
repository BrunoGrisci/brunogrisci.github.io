About me

Professor at the Theoretical Informatics Department of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul. I am a Computer Science PhD with expertise in Artificial Intelligence and Bioinformatics from the Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS), Brazil, with MSc and BSc in Computer Science at the same institution. I completed four study abroad periods at the University of Birmingham (BSc), the Universidad de Santiago de Chile (USACH) (MSc), the Karlsruher Institut für Technologie (KIT) (PhD), and the Dalhousie University (PhD) through the Emerging Leaders in the Americas Program. I am a member of the AlumNode.org network. My main research focus is on Machine Learning, Bioinformatics, and Evolutionary Computation. Outside the academia, I have experience as freelancer journalist and writer. I worked as Research Assistant II at Universidade do Vale do Rio dos Sinos (Unisinos) in a project with Dell Inc. and as Adjunct Assistant Professor at the Theoretical Computer Science Department of UFRGS. I was one of 200 young researchers in computing and mathematics invited to the 7th Heidelberg Laureate Forum in 2019, and my PhD thesis received honors from the PPGC-UFRGS, the Young Bioinformatician Award for best thesis from the Brazilian Association of Bioinformatics and Computational Biology (AB3C), and an Honorable Mention in the 2024 CAPES Thesis Award in the field of Computing. Grantee of the Public Call no. 8 by Instituto Serrapilheira.

ExpLAIn

The ExpLAIn — Explainability Laboratory for Artificial Intelligence is a research group led by Prof. Bruno Iochins Grisci at the Institute of Informatics (INF), Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS), in Porto Alegre, Brazil. We investigate methods and tools to make AI more interpretable, transparent, and trustworthy, with research spanning interpretable machine learning, relevance visualization and aggregation, biomarker discovery & AI for biology, and evolutionary optimization and neuroevolution. Our mission is to explain how models learn, decide, and fail, and translate that understanding into scientific and societal impact.
